On Page Optimization

On-page Optimization is also referred to as on-page SEO. It is basically a process in which individual web pages are optimized technically. This practice is a continuous process and is usually done so as to achieve maximum relevant traffic on a particular page of a website and to rank the website on the top in the google search results.

What factors impact On-Page SEO?

Content

The search position completely depends upon the quality and quantity of content a web page has. The reason is simple. If the content is easy, simple, attractive, and helpful for the page visitor it is going to reduce the bounce rate and increase the performance and hence the ranking. From an SEO point of view, good content is the one that is linkable and that which is easy to understand. Importantly the textual content with the right keywords results in good on-page SEO.

Image Alt-text

This is another very important aspect of on-page SEO. Alt-text is something that gives an identity to the images on your website. So, if a user is trying to find an image or information about something (something that you have on your website with a right Alt-text), it becomes easy for the user to reach out to your website.

Title Tags

Title Tags are an HTML element that actually defines the title of a particular web page. The title tags are clickable links that appear on the search results for the users. Therefore, the title tags have to be user-friendly, easy to understand, and open for sharing. To make it possible it is important to have a short and effective description of the page content so that the user would want to click on the title tag and explore further.

URLs

There are multiple URLs of your website. As the page changes, the URL changes too. It is important to have the hierarchy of the page in the URL while designing them. The paths need to be specified in the URLs so that it becomes more user friendly. Also, internal linking on a website plays a vital role in on-page SEO.
